# Case Study 02 — Aligned but Mediocre

## Scenario
A team that **agrees** things are mid-range — not bad enough to escalate, not
good enough to feel proud of. This exercises the case where high alignment
should NOT be confused with healthy: everyone is reporting the same lukewarm
experience. Interventions for this team look different from the polarised
case (Case 03), even though both can superficially read as "needs attention".

## Organisation
- **Industry:** Mid-market accounting firm
- **Team:** Audit team B
- **Department slug:** `audit-b`
- **N respondents:** 10 (1 partner, 3 seniors, 6 staff auditors)

## Conflict signature being tested
- Means cluster around 5–6 across most dimensions (mediocre)
- Low variance → r_wg ≥0.7 (still aligned)
- Booleans mostly 0 with a couple of 1s on cp08
- No split items, no subgroups
- Story: not a fractured team, just a stuck one
