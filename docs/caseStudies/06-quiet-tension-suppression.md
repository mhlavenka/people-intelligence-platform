# Case Study 06 — Quiet Tension (suppression pattern)

## Scenario
A team that **reports low conflict frequency** but also reports **low
psychological safety**. The classic suppression pattern: tension is going
underground rather than being resolved. People aren't fighting — but
they aren't speaking up either. This is one of the most diagnostically
important patterns in the instrument because the headline (low
"Conflict Frequency") looks healthy on its own.

The AI prompt should call out this combination explicitly: low cp07/cp09
with low cp01/cp02 means *suppression*, not *resolution*.

## Organisation
- **Industry:** Professional services (law firm)
- **Team:** Litigation associates
- **Department slug:** `litigation`
- **N respondents:** 10 (all associates, mix of seniority)

## Conflict signature being tested
- cp07 (tension frequency) and cp09 (productivity impact) cluster **low** (3–4 range)
- cp01, cp02 (psych safety) cluster **low** (3–5 range) and slightly divergent
- cp04, cp05, cp06 (comm/trust) middling and divergent
- cp08 (unresolved conflict): mostly 0 — people aren't naming an unresolved conflict
- cp12 (escalate to HR): mostly 0 — but one or two 1s
- cp14 (wellbeing): mid-low and divergent
- AI narrative: explicit "suppression risk" framing

## Expected metric outcomes
| Signal | Expected |

| Risk score | 50–60 (Medium-High) |
